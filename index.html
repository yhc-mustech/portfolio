About
Composition Electroacoustic Music Technology East Asian Music Music Theory Scholarly Research

Yu-Hsin Chang is a Taiwanese composer-performer and music technologist whose work explores the intersection of contemporary classical composition and East Asian musical heritage. Drawing on electroacoustic techniques, extended performance practice, and experimental methods, her music traces the metamorphosis of sonic tradition—reconstructing aesthetic and symbolic meaning through sound, texture, and pulse.

Her research engages with interactive music programming, augmented improvisation, immersive sound spatialization, and the dissemination of contemporary East Asian music. She holds a Ph.D. in Composition and Music Theory from the University of California, Davis, and currently serves as Assistant Professor at the Academy of Music, Macau University of Science and Technology.

Her compositions have been performed at international festivals and venues including the Asian Composers League, International Society for Contemporary Music (ISCM), Earplay concert series, C-Lab Taiwan Sound Lab, Etchings Festival, Mise-En Music Festival, Cortona Sessions, and Shanghai Conservatory New Music Week, with support from the National Culture and Arts Foundation of Taiwan.
